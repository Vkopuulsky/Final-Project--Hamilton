About Hamilton/Schuyler

Alexander Hamilton (1757-1804)

Alexander Hamilton was a delegate to the Constitutional Convention and a major author of the Federalists papers, he was the United States' first secretary of treasury. He was also a founding father and is now the face on the $10 bill.

Angelica Schuyler (1756-1814)

Angelica Schuyler was daughter of Phillip Schuyler and Catherine Van Rensselaer Schuyler, sister of Eliza Schuyler and Peggy Schuyler, and close friend of Alexander. She married John Barker Church and became part of the social elite.

Eliza Schuyler (1757-1854)

Elizabeth Schuyler was the wife of Alexander Hamilton and middle sister to Angelica Schuyler and Peggy Schuyler. She had 8 children:Philip, Angelica, Alexander, James, John, William, Elizabeth, and Philip(not the same one as before.)

Peggy Schuyler (1758-1801)

Margarita Schuyler Van Rensselaer was the third daughter of Philip Schuyler and Catherine Schuyler. She married Stephen Van Rensslaer III. She was the sister in law of John Barker Church and Alexander Hamilton.

The Revolutionaries (Hamilton's Friends)

John Laurens (1754-1782)

John Laurens was an American soldier and a statesman from South Carolina. He was ranked Lieutenant colonel and fought in the Battle of Brandywine, Battle of Germantown, Battle of Monmouth, Battle of Coosawhatchie, Seige of Savannah, Siege of Yorktown, Battle of the Combahee River.

Hercules Mulligan (1740-1825)

Hercules Mulligan was a tailor and a spy during the American Revolutionary War. He went to Princeton college in New York. He was a secret agent for George Washington and he married Elizabeth Sanders. Hercules had 3 sons and 5 daughters.

Aaron Burr (1756-1836)

Aaron Burr was an American politician and served as vice president of the United States during President Thomas Jefferson's first term(1801-1805). He also served as the Continental Army officer in the Revolutionary War. Burr was also a lawyer and elected to the New York Assembly. He was married to Eliza Jumel. His only child was his daughter, Theodosia Burr Alston, named after her mother Theodosia Bartow Prevost.

Marquis De Lafayette (1757-1834)

Marquis De Lafayette was a French aristocrat and a military officer who fought in the American Revolutionary War. He was a key figure in the French Revolution and the July Revolution. He fought as a major general in America and a lieutnant general in France. He was commonly called The Hero of the Two Worlds.
